## Why Link a PDF to a QR Code?

PDF remains the most universal document format. Linking a QR code to a PDF gives instant access to:

- **Restaurant menus**: full version with photos
- **Sales brochures**: detailed product catalog
- **User manuals**: device instructions
- **Price lists**: updated pricing
- **Resumes**: full version of your career history
- **Terms and conditions**: legal documents

The advantage: the QR code is small (a sticker will do) but gives access to a multi-page document.

## Prerequisite: Host Your PDF Online

The QR code points to a URL. Your PDF must be accessible via a public link.

### Google Drive (Recommended)
1. Upload your PDF to Google Drive
2. Right-click → Share → "Anyone with the link"
3. Copy the link

<InfoBox type="tip">
For a direct link (that opens the PDF without the Google Drive interface), replace `/file/d/ID/view` with `/uc?export=download&id=ID` in the URL.
</InfoBox>

### Dropbox
1. Upload the PDF
2. Click "Copy link"
3. Replace `dl=0` with `dl=1` for a direct download

### Your Website
Upload the PDF to your hosting and use the direct URL (e.g., `https://mysite.com/docs/brochure.pdf`).

## Tip: Update Without Reprinting

If you keep the **same URL** and replace the PDF file at that address, the QR code will automatically point to the new version.

With a useqraft Pro account, you can also create a **dynamic** QR code whose destination URL can be changed at any time.

## Best Practices

1. **Optimize PDF size**: compress it for fast loading on mobile
2. **Make it responsive**: ensure text is readable on a phone screen
3. **Test the link**: scan the QR code from another device before distributing
4. **Add a call-to-action**: "Scan to view our catalog"
