## QR Codes in Education

Education is fertile ground for QR codes. They create a bridge between physical materials (textbooks, displays, handouts) and digital resources (videos, exercises, websites).

## In the Classroom

### Enriched Course Materials
A QR code in a handout links to an explanatory video, interactive animation, or online exercise. The paper handout becomes a gateway to multimedia resources.

### Exercises and Quizzes
QR code linking to a Google Form, Kahoot, or any quiz platform. Students scan and answer directly on their phones.

### Classroom Library
Each book features a QR code linking to a summary, comprehension questions, or reviews from other students.

### Student Portfolio
QR code on displayed work that links to the student's full online portfolio.

## School-Wide

### Parent Communication
QR codes in the student planner linking to the school website, parent portal, or important dates on the school calendar.

### Signage
QR codes in hallways linking to the school map, cafeteria hours, and school rules.

### School Events
Event QR codes for the school fair, parent-teacher meetings, and field trips.

## In Professional Training

### Technical Manuals
QR codes linking to video tutorials for technical skills (welding, cooking, mechanics). Learners can rewatch the demonstration as many times as needed.

### Certifications
QR code on the training certificate linking to an authenticity verification page.

### Supplementary Resources
PDF documents, article links, bibliographies — all accessible in a single scan.

<InfoBox type="info">
Teaching tip: create an educational treasure hunt with text QR codes hidden around the school. Each QR code contains a clue or a question. Students scan, answer, and move on to the next one.
</InfoBox>
